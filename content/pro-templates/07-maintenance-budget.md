## 3. Cost Category Definitions

### What Goes Where: Cost Allocation Guide

#### LABOR (Internal Workforce)

**Include:**
- Base wages for maintenance staff
- Overtime pay for maintenance activities
- Shift differentials
- On-call pay/standby allowances
- Temporary/contract maintenance workers on your payroll

**Exclude:**
- Benefits (typically HR budget)
- Production operator wages
- Engineering salaries
- Outsourced contractor labor (see Contractors)

**Allocation Tips:**
- Track by work order for accurate job costing
- Separate PM labor from CM (corrective) labor
- Charge to asset/cost center when possible

---

#### SPARE PARTS & MATERIALS

**Include:**
- Replacement parts (bearings, seals, belts, filters)
- Consumables (lubricants, cleaning supplies, rags)
- Raw materials for fabrication/repair
- Fasteners, gaskets, fittings
- Electrical components (fuses, switches, sensors)
- Pneumatic/hydraulic components

**Exclude:**
- Capital equipment (see CAPEX)
- Production raw materials
- Office supplies
- Tools (see Tools & Equipment)

**Allocation Tips:**
- Issue from storeroom against work orders
- Track critical spares separately
- Monitor slow-moving/obsolete inventory

---

#### CONTRACTOR SERVICES

**Include:**
- Specialized repair services
- OEM service technicians
- Inspection services (elevators, cranes, pressure vessels)
- Calibration services
- Annual shutdown contract labor
- Equipment rental with operator
- Consulting/engineering support

**Exclude:**
- Equipment rental without labor (see Tools)
- Construction projects (typically CAPEX)
- IT contractors (IT budget)

**Allocation Tips:**
- Get detailed invoices showing labor vs parts
- Track contractor performance/cost per job
- Compare contractor costs to internal capability

---

#### TOOLS & EQUIPMENT

**Include:**
- Hand tools and power tools
- Diagnostic equipment
- Test instruments
- Safety equipment (PPE)
- Tool storage and organization
- Equipment rental (without operator)
- Small equipment under capitalization threshold

**Exclude:**
- Items over capitalization threshold (see CAPEX)
- Production equipment
- Spare parts

**Allocation Tips:**
- Track tool inventory and condition
- Consider tool crib management
- Budget for replacement cycles

---

#### TRAINING & DEVELOPMENT

**Include:**
- Technical training courses
- Certification programs
- Safety training (OSHA, lockout/tagout)
- CMMS training
- Conference registration fees
- Travel for training (if not separate budget)
- Training materials and subscriptions

**Exclude:**
- General company training (HR)
- College tuition reimbursement (HR)
- Vendor training included in equipment purchase

---

#### SOFTWARE & CMMS

**Include:**
- CMMS subscription/license fees
- Mobile maintenance app licenses
- Predictive maintenance software
- Asset management software
- Integration and customization
- Annual support/maintenance fees

**Exclude:**
- General IT software (IT budget)
- ERP system costs (Finance/IT)
- One-time implementation (often CAPEX)

---

#### SAFETY & COMPLIANCE

**Include:**
- Regulatory inspection fees
- Compliance testing
- Environmental permits/fees
- Safety audits
- Fire suppression system testing
- Backflow preventer testing
- Code compliance upgrades (under cap threshold)

**Exclude:**
- Major compliance projects (CAPEX)
- General safety training (see Training)
- PPE (see Tools)

---

#### CONTINGENCY

**Include:**
- Unplanned emergency repairs
- Price escalation buffer
- Unforeseen requirements

**Guidelines:**
- Typically 5-10% of total budget
- Track utilization to calibrate future years
- Require approval for contingency spending

## Appendix: Excel/Spreadsheet Setup Tips

### Formulas for Budget Tracking

```
Variance = Budget - Actual
Variance % = (Budget - Actual) / Budget * 100
YTD Total = SUM(Jan:Current Month)
Forecast = YTD Actual + (Monthly Avg * Remaining Months)
Cost % of RAV = Annual Maint Cost / Replacement Asset Value * 100
Cost per Unit = Annual Maint Cost / Annual Production Units
```
