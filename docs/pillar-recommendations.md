# PILLAR 1: Business & Management

## Quick Wins (alle levels)
1. **Documenteer de huidige staat** - Zelfs als er geen formele strategie is, leg vast wat er wél is
2. **Bereken downtime costs** - Eén getal dat aandacht krijgt van management
3. **Start met 3-5 KPIs** - Begin klein, meet consistent
4. **Maak een asset criticality top-10** - Begin met de belangrijkste equipment

## Per Maturity Level

### Innocent (≤35%) → Aware
**Prioriteit: Bewustwording creëren**

| Actie | Tijdsframe | Investering |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Maintenance awareness sessie met management | 1 maand | Laag |
| Basis downtime tracking starten | 1-2 maanden | Laag |
| Asset register opzetten (start met kritieke assets) | 2-3 maanden | Medium |
| Eerste KPI dashboard maken | 2-3 maanden | Laag |

**Typical barriers:**
- Management ziet maintenance als "noodzakelijk kwaad"
- Geen data beschikbaar
- Reactieve cultuur ("we hebben geen tijd om proactief te zijn")

**How to overcome:**
- Focus op financiële impact (€ per uur downtime)
- Start klein, bewijs waarde met quick wins
- Zoek een sponsor in het management

### Aware (36-50%) → Developing
**Prioriteit: Structuur en processen**

| KPI framework uitbreiden | 1-2 maanden | Laag |
| Maandelijkse management reviews starten | Ongoing | Laag |

|-------|------------|-------------|

| Risk-based budgettering implementeren | 4-6 maanden | Medium |
| Life-cycle cost analyses voor kritieke assets | 6-12 maanden | Hoog |
| Maintenance in business case processen integreren | 3-6 maanden | Medium |

### Competent (71-85%) → Excellent
**Prioriteit: World-class en benchmark leadership**

| Actie | Tijdsframe | Investering |
|-------|------------|-------------|

# Industry-Specific Considerations

## Oil & Gas (Gulf States Focus)

### Regulatory Context
- **ADNOC HSSE requirements** (UAE)
- **Saudi Aramco SAES standards** (Saudi Arabia)
- **API standards** (global)
- Turnaround/shutdown cycles impact all planning

### Typical Challenges
1. **High contractor dependency** - Knowledge retention issue
2. **Aging infrastructure** - 30-40 year old facilities
3. **Remote locations** - Parts availability, specialist access
4. **Nationalization programs** - Skill development urgency
5. **Seasonal demand variation** - Summer peak loads

### Priority Recommendations
1. **P3 Equipment Reliability** - Highest impact given asset intensity
2. **P4 Organization** - Nationalization and knowledge transfer
3. **P1 Business** - TCO awareness for aging assets

## Petrochemical

### Typical Challenges
1. **Process complexity** - Multiple unit operations
2. **Quality sensitivity** - Equipment condition → product quality
3. **Environmental compliance** - Leak detection, emissions

### Priority Recommendations
1. **P2 Process Reliability** - OEE and quality interface
2. **P3 Equipment Reliability** - Rotating equipment, heat exchangers
3. **P5 Work Management** - Turnaround excellence

## Manufacturing - Process

### Typical Challenges
1. **OEE focus** - Availability, Performance, Quality all matter
2. **TPM expectations** - Autonomous maintenance culture
3. **Lean manufacturing alignment** - Waste reduction

### Priority Recommendations
1. **P2 Process Reliability** - OEE is primary metric
2. **P5 Work Management** - Planning efficiency
3. **P4 Organization** - TPM culture development
